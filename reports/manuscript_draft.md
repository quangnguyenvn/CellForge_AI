# Draft Manuscript: Circular Natural-Graphite Anodes with Bio-Based Conductive Surface Repair

## Abstract

We propose and evaluate a research direction based on `real:H003`: Green-purified or regenerated natural graphite can recover high anode performance when paired with thin bio-derived carbon or conductive polymer coatings that stabilize SEI formation and electronic pathways. The draft uses only validation-approved evidence items from the CellForge evidence queue.

## Introduction

Sustainable lithium-ion battery manufacturing requires electrode materials and processes that reduce hazardous chemistry while preserving electrochemical performance. The validated evidence in this draft focuses on a circular graphite-anode route.

## Evidence Synthesis

- `real:C099_01`: The carbon-coated graphite (G@C) prepared via a green tannic acid/formaldehyde self-assembly process achieves a higher specific capacity and stability during long-term cycling, retaining 89% capacity after 500 cycles at 2C. Metric: capacity retention = `89%` under 500 cycles at 2C rate, EC:DMC:EMC (1:1:1 vol%) + 1% VC electrolyte.
- `real:C107_01`: Non-thermal plasma (NTP) treatment serves as a chemical-free, low-temperature, and highly energy-efficient purification method capable of upgrading natural graphite purity to over 99.99%. Metric: carbon purity = `exceeding 99.99%` under Low bulk temperature, non-thermal plasma activation.
- `real:C107_02`: Spent graphite regenerated using a conductive polymer coating (HOS-PFM) achieves a high reversible capacity of 353 mAh/g at 0.1C with a Coulombic efficiency of 99.93% and improved capacity retention. Metric: reversible capacity = `353 mAh/g` under 0.1C charge/discharge rate, coated with conductive HOS-PFM polymer.
- `real:C110_01`: Biodegradable PHBV can successfully substitute PVDF as a binder for graphite anodes in lithium-ion batteries, delivering highly stable cycling with a specific capacity of 357 mAh/g and 99.1% capacity retention after 100 cycles. Metric: capacity retention = `99.1 %` under Graphite anode with 10 wt% PHBV binder, cycled at 36 mA/g over 100 cycles.

## Contradictions and Risk Controls

- `real:C110_01` risk: The processing required chloroform (CHCl3) solvent, and the polymer has lower adhesion to copper than PVDF.

## Proposed Experiment Plan

Compare natural graphite, regenerated spent graphite, and synthetic graphite baselines after non-thermal plasma purification and tannic-acid-derived carbon coating. Measure purity, ICE, SEI impedance, reversible capacity, 2C cycling retention, and process energy/chemical burden.
